The Xian Incident

By Brian Flaherty

Listen to the Music

We are woken in long-dead night.
Crickets stilled, the silent planets
altered in their course. The wind
wishing its spirit through shuttered windows.
Urgent, angry voices carry across
the compound, a wall map
flaring into coloured campaign
before the dancing torches.
Rising you dislodge a vase
blue shards petalled across the stone
and slippered, flee the chamber
past Nine Dragon Pool, the white
leaf currents of winter.

famous breast thrown free from your gown.
